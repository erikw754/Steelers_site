"Iron" Mike Webster

Full NameMichael Lewis Webster

BornMarch 18, 1952

Height6 ft 1 in (1.85 m)

CollegeWisconsin

RoleCenter

ABOUT

"Iron Mike" Mike Webster was the Pittsburgh Steelers’ fifth-round selection and the 125th player taken in the 1974 NFL Draft. For two years, he split time at center with veteran Ray Mansfield while seeing some service at guard and the special teams. However, with a start in the final game of the 1975 season, Webster began a string of 150 consecutive starts that lasted until 1986, when he missed the first four games with a dislocated elbow.


Webster played more seasons (15) and more games (220) than any other player in Pittsburgh history. Webster, who was the team’s offensive captain for nine seasons, was considered to be the strongest Steeler and won the Ironman competition in 1980 to give credence to that belief.



Webster, who joined the team in the same year the Steelers won their first of four Super Bowls, also played in six AFC championship games. Pittsburgh won four of the six title games. Webster was an all-pro choice seven times and was selected to the All-AFC team five times from 1978 through 1982. He also played in nine Pro Bowls, the first five as a starter.
