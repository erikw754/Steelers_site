B. J. Finney

Full NameBenjamin J. Isaac Finney II

BornOctober 26, 1991

Height6 ft 4 in (1.93 m)

College Kansas State

RoleOffensive Guard

ABOUT

The Pittsburgh Steelers signed Finney as an undrafted rookie free agent immediately following the 2015 NFL Draft. Even though he was a highly sought after undrafted free agent, he opted to sign with the Pittsburgh Steelers as they were his favorite NFL team as a child. Finney's surprising fall in the draft was said to be in part due to teams' fearing his arms were too short and that he lacked technique and athleticism.


Finney entered his training camp in 2016 competing for the job to be backup offensive guard or center. He was named the third string offensive guard to begin his second season and the third string center. On January 18, 2016, the Steelers signed him to a reserve/future contract.



Finney entered the 2018 season as a backup guard. He played in all 16 games. On March 6, 2019, the Steelers placed a second-round restricted free agent tender on Finney. On March 23, 2020, Finney signed a two-year, $8 million contract with the Seattle Seahawks. On October 28, 2020, Finney and a seventh-round draft pick in the 2021 NFL Draft were traded to the Cincinnati Bengals. Finney was placed on the reserve/COVID-19 list by the Bengals and was placed on the reserve/non-football injury list on December 31, 2020. Finney was released then signed a one-year contract with the Pittsburgh Steelers on March 12, 2021.
